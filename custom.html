Learning


The AP Program is recognized internationally, with over twenty-two thousand schools registered, and over one million graduates in total. The program encourages its participants to develop their skills in an environment more challenging than usual classes.

Students who study in AP classes study to prepare for university-level courses, starting right from the beginning of their junior year. Pre-AP begins in grades 9 and 10, which prepares them for the The exams that are given by the end of their 12th grade are accepted by universities and colleges worldwide.

Students who pass the AP Exam with more than or equal to a mark of 3 (approx. 60%) have a chance to skip the lowest course in that area of study, if that school allows it.

The program, of course, follows every areas' cirriculum, but is altered in a way that benefits the students so that they learn more than they usually would.

Each AP Exam is given out by the school's head of department (or the person with the highest authority) and marked by faculty members anonymously. The exam itself is made by the math specialists in the Advanced Placement company, and is reviewed by teachers in the University of Silicon, the prestegious university that this company is situated in.

Opportunities


The Advanced Placement Program offers many advantages with its students.

One of these benefits is the introduction of segregated (apart from traditional) classes, with experienced teachers that have been taught AP-course specifics in order to provide students with a supportive and challenging atmosphere to test their own limits with the resources we give to them.

Another is the chance to receive an extra credit for beginner-level university courses in that field, saving you precious time and money.

The AP Exam gives students something more to pursue in their course, and the another thing: its optional, meaning that students can decline the AP Exam if they are too stressed or overwhelmed.


Applications


Students can apply for the Advanced Placement Program in their school (if their school is participating) during the months of June, July, and August. They could also apply for our tutoring program if their school is not participating.
